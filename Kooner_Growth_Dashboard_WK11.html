🔍 Root Cause Framework

When Kooner is not getting the work, the cause falls into one of two buckets. The response is different for each.

⚡ Performance Gap

Response time, repair quality, completion rate, pre-auth process. DSP stopped calling because of a friction point. Fix: operational improvement.

🤝 Relationship Gap

DSP had a bad experience, drifted to another vendor, or never had a strong connection to begin with. Fix: direct outreach, accountability, trust-building.

Key: Diagnose which bucket each station falls into before deciding the response. Sending a relationship pitch to a performance problem — or vice versa — won't work.

Strategic Actions — WK11
These are investment and capability decisions that require planning, resources, or leadership sign-off. They set the conditions for long-term growth and cannot be solved by a phone call alone.
🔴 High Impact — Decide Now
HVAC Capability
Assess HVAC certification at HDT3 and HCL2. 8 ROs leaked to HVAC-capable vendors this week alone. Summer volume will increase this. Decision needed: do Kooner's current techs at these stations have HVAC certification? If not, what is the plan and timeline to add it?
If HVAC gap is confirmed — add a certified tech or pursue certification for an existing tech at HDT3 and HCL2. This is a recurring revenue unlock across at least 4 stations.
Pre-Authorization Process
Build a formal pre-auth SOP. The FICL dispute at HAU1 is a symptom of a process gap. A clear, written protocol — when to seek pre-auth, how to escalate urgently, what happens if FMC denies — needs to exist and be trained across all stations.
Audit all denied/disputed ROs in the last 8 weeks across all stations. Quantify the financial and relationship cost of pre-auth failures to build the business case for the SOP investment.
🟠 Medium-Term — Plan & Resource
Road Service Expansion
Survey current after-hours and road service capacity across all stations. 6 emergency events went to competitors in WK11. The vendor who responds to breakdowns becomes the trusted default for everything else.
Evaluate a limited on-call rotation at HLA2, HDT3, and HMS2 — the three highest-leakage stations. Even extended business hours (not 24/7) would differentiate Kooner from competitors who only operate M–F.
If road service capacity exists today — communicate it immediately to ARMA, SFRM, LDOG, JDTC, and JBXL. This is a zero-cost win if the capability already exists but is not being marketed.
PM Agreement Program
Develop a structured PM agreement offering for DSPs. 36% of leakage is scheduled maintenance — PM combos that DSPs are routing to vendors with whom they have standing agreements. Kooner needs a formal, repeatable PM program to offer, not just ad-hoc pitches.
Use Fleet IQ vehicle data to build proactive PM schedules for LDOG, JBXL, and SFRM — arrive at the pitch with a ready-built plan based on their actual fleet mileage and service history. This demonstrates capability and reduces friction for DSPs to say yes.
🔵 Long-Term — Build the Foundation
HPD1 Turnaround
Develop a formal HPD1 station recovery plan with a 90-day target. At 9.1% adoption this is an account rescue, not a quick fix. It needs a dedicated owner, a written plan, weekly progress tracking, and executive visibility.
Assess and close the diagnostics capability gap at HPD1. Ford-heavy fleet with recurring CEL / OBD work requires scan tools and trained techs. No relationship repair will stick if the capability to do the work isn't there.
Measurement & Accountability
Add Growth & Expansion as a standing WBR agenda item. Leakage recovery needs to be reviewed weekly alongside adoption — the two metrics tell the same story from opposite angles.
Set a WK11 recovery target for each critical station (HLA2, HDT3, HMS2, HPD1) and track progress weekly in this dashboard. Without a target, there is no accountability.
Build a DSP health scorecard — track adoption trend, last outreach date, relationship status, and open issues for every DSP across all stations. Proactive management prevents the kind of full disengagement seen at HPD1.

📌 Guiding Principles
Diagnose before acting

Every leaking station needs a root cause diagnosis — performance gap or relationship gap — before the response is decided. The wrong fix wastes time and can make things worse.

Fix capability first

HVAC, brake/air system, and diagnostics gaps need to be closed operationally before DSP outreach on those repair types can succeed. A pitch without the capability to back it up erodes trust further.

Measure progress weekly

WK11 is the baseline: 58 ROs, 9 stations. Pull leakage data every Monday and track whether the number is moving. Stations where outreach is active should show improvement within 2–3 weeks.
